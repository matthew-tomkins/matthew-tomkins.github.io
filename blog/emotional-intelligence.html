EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE

EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE: WHAT IS IT?

What exactly is Emotional Intelligence? Emotional Intelligence (or EI) is basically your ability to handle your own emotions and understand what's going on with other people emotionally. Think of it like strong people skills with a conscious awareness of the feelings behind everything.

THE 5 KEY ELEMENTS

There are five main elements that make up Emotional Intelligence:

  • Self-awareness - knowing what you're feeling and why
  • Self-regulation - managing your emotions instead of letting them run wild
  • Motivation - staying driven and positive
  • Empathy - understanding how others are feeling
  • Social skills - being good at interacting with people

Dr. Josh Garrin from Southern New Hampshire University sums the concept up well: "Emotional intelligence enables us to ‘walk a mile in someone’s shoes,’ which might just be the most important human skill of all”.

UNDERSTANDING OTHERS (AND YOURSELF)

You can't control how other people feel or act, but what you can do is try to figure out what emotions are driving their behaviour. Once you understand where someone's coming from emotionally, it's much easier to navigate how to interact more successfully with them.

This applies to yourself too. When you take a moment to understand your own reactions and think about how they might affect others, you're more likely to have an actual conversation rather than finding yourself in conflict.

Emotional intelligence helps you step back from your own perspective and consider how things might look from someone else's point of view. The better you get at this, the more you can identify, manage, and even predict emotional responses - both yours and other people's.

IT'S NOT ABOUT BEING OVERLY SENSITIVE

Having high emotional intelligence doesn't mean you're super sensitive or emotional all the time. You might feel things more deeply, and you'll probably be more empathetic, but the key difference is that you're better at managing those emotions rather than being overwhelmed by them.

The more emotionally intelligent you become, the better you get at understanding feelings - yours and everyone else's.

HOW TO GET BETTER AT IT

Good news! You can work on improving your emotional intelligence with some practice. Here are a few things that will help to develop this skill:

  • Slow down your reactions - Don't just react immediately when something triggers you. Take a breath.
  • Know your strengths and weaknesses - Be honest about what you're good at and what you need to work on.
  • Pay attention to non-verbal communication - People communicate so much through body language, tone of voice, and what they don't say.
  • Work on clear, open communication - Be genuine about how you express yourself and really listen to others.

It's not an impossible skill to develop, but it does take intentional effort. The more you practice thinking about emotions - yours and others - the more natural it becomes.
