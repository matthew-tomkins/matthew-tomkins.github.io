NEUROPLASTICITY & GROWTH MINDSET

WHAT IS NEUROPLASTICTY

Neuroplasticity enables new skill development by creating and strengthening neurological pathways through practice, repetition, and connection to existing knowledge and emotions. The more these pathways are used, the stronger they become.

There's almost no limit to what can be developed, depending on mindset and dedication. Celebrating personal progress is essential - everyone has unique natural abilities, so measure growth against your own journey rather than comparing yourself to others when pursuing new skills, knowledge, and emotional development.

The four key principles that drive successful neuroplasticity application are best expressed using the acronym A.G.E.S:

  • ATTENTION - The ability to engage and focus on what is being learned or developed
  • GENERATION - Creating emotional connections and meaningful associations with existing knowledge or skills, including reflecting on your own learning process
  • EMOTION - Attaching emotion to learning through rewards or challenges that push comfort zones significantly increases information retention
  • SPACING - Distributing learning over time with repetition and retrieval, rather than cramming, strengthens retention and transfers knowledge from short-term to long-term memory

GROWTH VS FIXED MINDSETS

To help develop neuroplasticity and its growth it is important to take a positive approach, and there are two mindsets - Growth and Fixed - that will help you succeed or hold you back.

A growth mindset sees beyond present limitations and embraces the motivation to tackle challenges. It embodies the belief: "If I work at this, I can achieve it" and "This isn't easy, but I know hard work will get me there!"

A fixed mindset avoids challenges and growth opportunities. Based in pessimism, it believes that if something isn't easy, it's too hard or impossible, leading to diminished motivation driven by self-doubt and disbelief in a persons capacity for mental development.

They are truly polar attitudes - growth mindset nurtures resilience and confidence, while fixed mindset’s can be restrictive and destructive of your growth. It is proven through many studies if you take a growth mindset toward challenges you are more likely to achieve your goal and excel.


PRAISE PROCESS NOT JUST TALENT

Recognise achievements that required hard work and weren't easily attained! Measure and celebrate your learning growth and application to build resilience and foster a growth mindset when you’re feeling challenged. Don't dismiss failure - use it as a measurement for genuine growth and development and reward effort, strategy, and progress.

Also, be aware that praising your immediate success can be discouraging and may reduce motivation and your growth mindset development. Instead, focussing on praising your progress and process encourages the learning journey and how to meet challenges. This approach promotes greater effort, diverse strategies, deeper engagement over time, and increased perseverance.

TOOLS TO BOOST YOUR GROWTH MINDSET

Brain-Healthy Diet

Your brain represents just 2% of your body weight but consumes 20% of your energy. Just as you'd fuel muscle growth at the gym with high-protein foods, your brain "muscle" needs proper nutrition to develop. Limiting excessive sugar and processed foods helps maintain mental clarity and prevents inflammation that undermines brain health.

Exercise

Physical activity increases blood flow to the brain while reducing stress and inflammation. These changes enhance mood, memory, focus, and processing speed, preserving cognitive function over time.

Quality Sleep

During sleep, your brain processes and stores information, eliminates toxins, and repairs neural pathways. Quality sleep is fundamental for optimal brain health.

Challenge Your Mind

Regular mental challenges strengthen neural pathways and support long-term brain health. Try mental puzzles, learn new skills like a different language, travel and explore, play an instrument, listen to new music, or engage in social activities.

Build Social Connections

Conversations and interactive activities stimulate essential mental processes like attention and memory. Regular social interaction reinforces neural connections, slowing age-related decline and potentially delaying dementia by strengthening cognitive reserve.

Stress Management

Chronic stress damages brain function by elevating cortisol levels, which can harm neurons and inhibit neuroplasticity. Effective stress management protects cognitive health and supports long-term brain function. Practice mindfulness meditation and pursue relaxing or engaging hobbies that release endorphins.

WHAT DOES NEUROPLASTICITY MEAN TO ME?

Neuroplasticity gives me confidence I can apply myself and achieve my goals through repetition, perseverance and motivated effort.

Incorporating a growth mindset as a flipped perspective on personal limitations and learning processes is crucial. As opposed to a fixed mindset (booooo!), a growth mindset recognises and celebrates challenges and difficulties as part of the process and not the final result. These challenges are and should be expected!

With application and a positive mindframe you are able to achieve your goals, and keep in mind working through challenges can potentially lead to a deeper understanding of skills, concepts and emotional growth.
