Looking back at my original assessment, my strengths remain the same but have grown stronger through Foundations. People skills and project management are definitely my core strengths. The remote work experience I mentioned has proven valuable for time management and self-discipline.

My main concern is staying focused and keeping up with the material and exercises through Bootcamp. I'm still feeling a bit of imposter syndrome - that gap between where I am now and where I feel I should be technically. The pace is going to ramp up significantly, and I want to make sure I don't fall behind or let that self-doubt creep in when things get challenging.

I've proven I can manage the Foundations pace, but Bootcamp will be on another level. Maintaining momentum and confidence when the workload intensifies will be my biggest hurdle.

HUMAN SKILLS I WANT TO DEVELOP

I want to continue developing confidence and self-belief using growth mindset as my foundation - this has been the most valuable tool from Foundations. When I hit technical walls (which I will), I need to keep that "ok, I don't get it right now but that's cool, I'll find the answer" mentality strong.

Better problem-solving application is key - not just getting through exercises, but really understanding the process so I can apply it to new challenges. I also want to get better at incorporating reflections to strengthen my learnings. The habit of stepping back and processing what worked, what didn't, and why needs to become more automatic.

EXPECTATIONS FROM THE BOOTCAMP TEAM

More of the same from Foundations - the support and positive community environment at Dev Academy has been awesome. The facilitation team has been responsive, kind and helpful which has provided me with a lot of growth.

I'm looking forward to working with the whole cohort rather than just my home group. Starting the course in the smaller home group Tree has been great and I feel I lucked into a truly lovely and supportive crew. EXPECTATIONS OF MYSELF IN BOOTCAMP

I'm committed to applying myself fully and working through challenges and not giving up when things get tough. I want to be a positive contribution to the cohort - someone who lifts others up and creates good energy in the group.

I'll use emotional intelligence to amplify productivity for myself and others. I've learned in previous professional roles that managing your own energy and supporting others energy is crucial for team success.

I'll manage my workload by staying organised, communicating clearly with the cohort and facilitators, and maintaining that balance between pushing through challenges and knowing when to reach out. The "power nap and breather" from my original plan will be so important when the intensity increases.

Most importantly, I'm committed to staying curious and maintaining that growth mindset when the technical challenges feel overwhelming. That's going to be the foundation that gets me through the next nine weeks and sets me up for success beyond Dev Academy.
