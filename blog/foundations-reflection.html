FOUNDATION REFLECTIONS

THREE NEW THINGS I'VE LEARNED ABOUT MYSELF AND MY EGO

The biggest shift has been consciously questioning my ego when it shows up - recognising when it's getting in the way of learning rather than helping. I've learnt that it's completely normal to find something difficult, and that difficulty doesn't mean impossibility. A lot of people experience it, but most aren’t open and share that difficulty with others. The key is catching myself when negative thoughts try to take over, and actively working through it rather than letting it shut me down.

Growth mindset has become a tool I incorporate daily rather than just a concept. When facing challenges now, I approach them with "ok, I don't get it right now but that's cool. I'll find the answer - I can do it" rather than immediately assuming something is beyond my capabilities. It's shifted from ego protection mode to genuine curiosity about what I need to do to solve this.

THE ROLE OF VALUES, EMPATHY, AND SELF-AWARENESS

They all work together as a package that nurtures growth, productivity and builds a positive community environment. Values keep you grounded in what matters when things get tough. Empathy helps you understand other perspectives and collaborate effectively - crucial when you're problem-solving with others or even just understanding how other people react in situations. Self-awareness lets you recognise your own patterns, strengths, and blind spots so you can work with them rather than against them. Together, they create the foundation for sustainable growth and meaningful contribution to any team or project.

WHAT SURPRISED ME MOST

Mindfulness caught me off guard - how much most days are just a blur with life and news constantly demanding attention. Taking a moment to actually be present and appreciate where you are right now is really powerful and important. Simple things like noticing a butterfly cruising past, birds catching worms, or acknowledging the physical feeling of warmth from the sun become meaningful when you slow down to actually experience them instead of automatically blocking them out.

It's such a simple practice but the impact has been amazing. It's like discovering there's this whole layer of richness in daily life that I was just rushing past without noticing.

THE MOST CHALLENGING ASPECTS

The hardest part has been taking time to genuinely reflect on and interpret my personal views and natural reactions. It's easy to agree with the Human Skills tools and concepts, but really questioning whether I actually apply them when things get tough is much more challenging. Looking honestly at my auto responses and patterns isn't always comfortable.

It requires slowing down and being really honest about how I actually behave versus how I think I behave. That gap between intention and reality can be pretty humbling, but that's where the real growth happens.

WHY FOCUS ON HUMAN SKILLS IN A PROGRAMMING COURSE?

It's core preparation for being ready to handle challenges. The course is setting up a "can do" ethos before we get hit with heavier workloads and serious mental obstacles. Programming isn't just about technical knowledge - it's about personal application, problem-solving under pressure, and maintaining momentum when things get difficult.

Human skills are what determine whether you push through the tough moments or give up when feeling challenged. Technical skills can be learnt, but the mindset to keep going when you're stuck is what actually gets you through the learning process and achieve results.

WAS THIS TIME WELL SPENT?

For sure! I've really enjoyed the Human Skills modules, and they've directly impacted my progress in a positive way already. Adjusting and embracing Growth Mindset has been the most useful tool when confronted with technical challenges and exercises.

The mentality to continue applying yourself and not give in to "it's too hard, it'll always be too hard for me" has been crucial. Coding concepts can be researched, repeated, and eventually conquered - but if you give up mentally, there's no potential to continue and grow. The human skills give you the resilience to stick with the technical learning when it can feel impossible in that moment, which is where the real progress and personal growth happens.
