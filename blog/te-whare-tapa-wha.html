TE WHARE TAPA WHĀ

THE FOUR DIMENSIONS OF WELLBEING

Back in 1984, Sir Mason Durie created Te Whare Tapa Whā - a Māori approach to health and wellbeing. The concept uses the metaphor of a wharenui (meeting house) standing strong on four pillars. When all four pillars are solid, the whole structure works - and that's how our hauora (wellbeing) functions.

TAHA TINANA - Physical Wellbeing

TAHA HINENGARO - Mental and Emotional Wellbeing

TAHA WAIRUA - Spiritual Wellbeing

TAHA WHĀNAU - Family and Social Wellbeing

WHENUA - Connection to Land

Dev Academy has consciously embraced this philosophy into its learning foundation as part of its commitment to Te Tiriti O Waitangi, honouring and integrating these principles. They value wellbeing and awareness at the heart of their learning journey and what truly represents our community in Aotearoa.

MY WELLBEING PLAN


TAHA TINANA (Physical Wellbeing)

Prioritize physical health by maintaining a consistent gym routine of at least three sessions weekly

Sustain healthy sleep patterns with regular bedtime and wake routines, ensuring minimum seven hours of quality rest

Prepare and enjoy fresh, nutritious meals throughout each day


TAHA HINENGARO (Mental and Emotional Wellbeing)

Practice daily mindfulness meditation and extend mindful awareness toward others in interactions

Regularly reflect on learning progress while maintaining balance between study and personal life, consciously managing stress levels


TAHA WAIRUA (Spiritual Wellbeing)

Remain open-minded and welcome challenges that expand perspective

Avoid discrimination while staying true to personal values, embracing new learnings that align with core beliefs


TAHA WHĀNAU (Family and Social Wellbeing)

Connect with Auckland family through weekly phone calls for check-ins and updates

Prioritise quality time with uncle and cousins in Christchurch, nurturing these meaningful reconnections with their growing families

Practice kindness and patience within the community—whether waiting in supermarket lines or navigating busy traffic


WHENUA (Connection to Land)

Stay mindful of the environment and appreciate daily moments—like greeting the neighbour’s cat during its yard visits or watching blackbirds in the backyard

Explore South Island's natural beauty during spare time, recognising these experiences as grounding and deeply fulfilling
